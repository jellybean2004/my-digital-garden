- the euler-lagrange equation: 
$$\frac{\partial L}{\partial q} - \frac{d}{dt} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot q} = 0$$
	where $q$ can be $x, \theta, s,\dots$
- the lagrangian, $L$, is a function of $q$ and $\dot q$, eg: $L(x,\dot x), L(\theta, \dot\theta)$
