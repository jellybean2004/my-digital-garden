- semiconductors at zero $T$ have filled outer band of electrons
- pauli exclusion principle means, if $E$ is applied, electrons have no energy levels to move into

## n- type semiconductors
- $Si$ is doped with a material with electrons just below the conduction band (eg: $P$)
- this increases the number of negative charge carriers
- electrons are easily excited to the conduction band
![Pasted image 20241015121226.png](/img/user/pics/Pasted%20image%2020241015121226.png)
## p-type semiconductors
- $Si$ is doped with a material with 'spare' spaces for electrons (eg: $B$) just above the valence band
- receptor sites from $B$ capture electrons, leaving a positive 'hole' in the valence band
- the hole behaves like a positive mobile charge, hence it is called a 'p-type' semiconductor
![Pasted image 20241015121430.png](/img/user/pics/Pasted%20image%2020241015121430.png)
## p-n junction
![Pasted image 20241015121829.png](/img/user/pics/Pasted%20image%2020241015121829.png)
- a junction with p-type on the left, and n-type on the right
- the free electrons diffuse to the left, and the free holes diffuse to the right
- this leaves charges behind, establishing an electric field
- reduction in the number of conduction charges creates a depletion zone
- the electric field set up by the motion of electrons and holes eventually reaches an equilibrium, preventing further motion of the charges
- if  photon with an energy $>\sim E_{G}$ is absorbed inside the depletion zone, it can create an electron-hole pair
- electron from this pair moves to the n-type, and the hole to the p-type, diving a current

## shockley-queisser limit
- radiation from the sun has a spectrum
- $Si$ band cap is $1.1\,eV$, so photons with an energy below this cannot generate electricity
![Pasted image 20241015122906.png](/img/user/pics/Pasted%20image%2020241015122906.png)
- black overlap is the fraction that is converted to electricity

- **improving efficiency:**
	- using materials tuned to the different parts of the solar spectrum
	- stack photovoltaic cells to increase the absorption in the depletion zone
