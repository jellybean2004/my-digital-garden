## efficiency
- idealized heat engine inputs heat, $Q_{in}$, generates mechanical work, $W$, and has an output heat, $Q_{out}$
$$
\eta = \frac{W}{Q_{in}}= \frac{Q_{in}-Q_{out}}{Q_{in}}
$$
#incomplete
## carnot efficiency
$$
\eta = 1- \frac{Q_{out}}{Q_{in}} = 1- \frac{T_{L}}{T_{H}}
$$
where, $T_H$ is temp of working media, $T_L$ is temp of exhaust $\phi_{out}$ phase

- carnot engine gives the maximum possible efficiency for converting heat to work
