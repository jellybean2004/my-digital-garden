## scalar
- *has magnitude but no direction*
- can be positive or negative
- eg: mass, time, speed
## vector
- *has magnitude and direction*
- eg: $\vec v, \vec p, \vec a, \vec F$
- in vector form:
$$\vec F = m \vec a = \frac{d \vec p}{dt}=m \frac{d \vec v}{dt}$$
	- $r = \frac{2}{\vec v}$    x
	- $m+\vec v = \vec p$    x
		- vector cannot equal scalars
